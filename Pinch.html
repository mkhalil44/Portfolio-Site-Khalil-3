Pinch

Introduction

Pinch is a mobile application that helps people take control of their finances. The main goal was to create an applicationthat people from all financial background can easily navigate and use. Everything from account balances to credit scores are all located in one place.

My Process

Research

By conducting extensive research into the potential competitor's of Pinch and performal several user interviews, I was able to better understand the market for money-saving and banking applications. The interview was made up of the following questions:

  • How old are you?
  • How long have you been using your current money-saving app?
  • Why do you use a money-saving app?
  • What are some features you enjoy about your current money-saving app?
  • What are some features you dislike about your current money-saving app?
  • What features would convince you to switch to a new money-saving app?

The interviews revealed that users wanted a simple way to set up financial goals and to take control of their savings.

Ideation

The data collected during the interview process allowed me to create six user stories and user flows which actions a user would take to accomplish a desired task. The user stories were divided into new customers and returning customers. Sucess criteria were created based off of the user stories.

User Stories:

  • "As a new customer, I want all of my account information in one place, so that I can easily track all of my spending." Sucess Criteria 1: Access to all bank accounts
  • "As a new customer, I want to know that my account is safe and secure, so that I don't have to worry about fraud." Success Criteria 2: Advanced security measures
  • "As a new customer, I want access to a free credit score and report in order to monitor my finances." Success Criteria 3: Access to a free credit score and report
  • "As a returning customer, I want notifications that alert me when my bills are due and to keep track of my spending." Success criteria 4: Billing and spending alerts
  • "As a returning customer, I want to be able to review previous conversations from financial advisors." Success Criteria 5: Summary reports from financial advisors
  • "As a returning customer, I want to be able to create and view all of my financial goals." Success Criteria 6: Create and view financial goals

Wireframe

After the research and ideation phase, I developed low-fidelity wireframes both by hand and using Balsamiq. The wireframes were created with the user stories in mind.

Design

Due to the personal and important nature of this application, the design was made to look clean, simple, and professional. One of the emotional design concepts I applied was to refer to the psychology of color and chose a hue that would be most suitable to the app. I chose dark blue because it symbolizes trust, loyalty, and wisdom, all of which are ideal to a money-saving application.

Prototype

I created a UseBerry scenario-based test to gain insight into user behaviors, such as click time and heatmap analysis. In addition, I conducted face-to-face tests through InVision Prototype to collect more data on user difficulty in completing the tasks. Users were asked to complete the following tasks in both tests"

  • Go to the expenses page.
  • Go to the goals page and create a new goal.
  • Check your credit score.
  • Go to the accounts page.
  • Go to the reports page and download a full PDF document.

The results showed that users were confused by the buttons on the home screen and didn't know whether to slide or click the buttons. Only 51% of users were able to successfully find the credit score page and some dropped off due to frustration. 100% of users were able to find the expenses page in under 10 seconds, while 80% of the users were able to find the goals page. All of those who did not drop off were able to find the accounts page and download a PDF from the reports page.

Conclusion

Although the first prototype showed that the majority of the users were able to navigate through the app successfully, it also revealed some problems. Based off of the data received from user testing, I have made a variety of changes to the icons and labels, which I am hoping will solve these issues. I also created an additional screen showing users how to slide the home screen buttons. The next step would be to conduct additional user testing to see if these changes have increased user success rates.
